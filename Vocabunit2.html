Unit 2 Vocab
Population Density A measure of the total population relative to land size.
Arithmetic Population Density Similar to population density, it measures the amount of people per square mile.
Physiological Population Density The amount of people per square area with productive agriculture.
Population Distribution The arrangement of people on Earths surface.
Dot Map A map that has many dots with each dot representing a certain amount of something and where it’s located.
Megalopolis A term that refers to massive urban collections or agglomerations.
Census A periodic or official count of a countries population. (Occurs every 10 years.)
Doubling Time The time needed for a population to double it’s size.
Population Explosion The rapid growth of human population before double time.
Natural Increase- Growth in the population with live births over deaths.
Crude Birth Rate The amount of births per year per thousand people.
Crude death rate The amount of deaths per year per thousand people.
Demographic Transition The changes in population growth dependent on industrialization.
Stationary Population level The level where a population begins to stop growing.
Population composition Structure of a population in terms of age, gender, martial status and education.
Population Pyramid A pyramid that shows the age percentages in a total population.
Infant mortality rate The number of deaths every year of infants one year or younger per thousand people.
Child mortality rate The amount of children within a certain population that die within one to five years of age.
Life expectancy The estimated time a person will live.
AIDS A disease that affects the immune system to the point where it weakens the immune system to allow other diseases to set in.
Chronic disease Long lasting afflictions because of higher life expectancy.
Expansive population policies Policies that are designed to favor families to raise the rate of the population growth.
Eugenic population policies Policies designed to favor one racial sector over others.
Restrictive population policies Policies designed to reduce the rate of natural population increase.
