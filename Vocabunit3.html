Unit 3 Vocab
Remittances Monies that Migrants send home to their families.
Cyclic Movements Shorter, regular trips away from home for a defined amount of time.
Nomadism A type of Cyclic Movement, based on a matter of survival, culture and traditions.
Periodic Movements A journey that starts at home, return home at a later date.
Migrant Labor On page 98 in the text book, Migrant Laborers are also known as Guest workers people from other countries coming to work in a different country. People who come from different countries and work for that country.
Transhumance Farmers that move livestock seasonally, usually higher elevation in summer, lower elevation in the winter.
Guest workers Guest workers and Migrant Laborers are the same thing. Guest workers are documented migrants that tend to serve for a short amount of time.
International Migration Movement between countries
Internal Migration Movement within the country.
Forced Migration Migration that is forced and there is no choice.
Voluntary Migration Occurs after a migrant has looked at their options even if desperately.
Laws of Migration 1. Most Migrants travel shorter distances. 2. Migrants who travel long distances usually choose big city destinations. 3. Rural to urban!! 4.Although rural to urban dominate, always counter migration streams. (Although people had to leave in new York to go to a smaller town they can still work the job they had in new York). 5. Single young adults more migratory than families.
Gravity Model The gravity model is a model that predicts the interaction between places based on the population size and the distance between them.
Push Factors Factors that would make people want to leave that area.
Pull factors Factors that would make people want to leave that area.
Distance Decay The further away from the country they came from, the less they will interact. The closer they are to their home country the more they will interact.
Step Migration Migration where a person moves to a new country and slowly gets used to the environment, with twists or steps along the way.
Intervening Opportunity An opportunity that is much closer than the place they want to go, as a result they’d rather stay there than go to where they are going.
Kinship Links You have migrated to where your family is, or family has migrated to where you live. 
Chain Migration The influence of a Migrant on others, bringing other migrants to the country.
Refugees Someone who is fleeing away from danger.
Internal Refugees Also known as Internally displaced persons. They are people who have been displaced in their country.
International Refugees People who are displaced but are forced to go to a different country.
Asylum The right for protection in the first country they arrive in.
Immigration laws Laws made to control Immigration into that country.
Quotas Like a treaty of some sort that limits how many Immigrants can come in the country.
Selective Immigration Only certain immigrants may come in, immigrants with bad backgrounds will be turned down.
